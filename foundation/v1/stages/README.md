# Engineering Stages

> Foundation v1

Engineering work progresses through a sequence of well-defined stages.

Each stage represents a specific objective in the software engineering lifecycle.

Stages are designed to reduce uncertainty by ensuring that every project follows the same execution model.

A stage is considered complete only when its exit criteria have been satisfied.

---

# Purpose

Stages organize engineering work into manageable, measurable, and repeatable units.

Rather than asking:

> "What should we do next?"

The Engineering Engine always knows the next stage.

---

# Design Principles

Every stage follows the same structure.

Each stage defines:

- Purpose
- Inputs
- Participants
- Activities
- Deliverables
- Exit Criteria
- Next Stage

Consistency is more valuable than complexity.

---

# Engineering Lifecycle

Every project follows the same lifecycle.

```
00 Project Zero

↓

01 Discovery

↓

02 Research

↓

03 Architecture

↓

04 Planning

↓

05 Development

↓

06 Testing

↓

07 Deployment

↓

08 Retrospective
```

Stages may evolve over time, but the lifecycle remains stable.

---

# Human + AI Collaboration

Each stage activates only the engineering roles required for that phase.

This minimizes unnecessary complexity while ensuring that every decision receives the appropriate technical perspective.

Participants are determined by the Engineering Engine.

Not by individual preference.

---

# Engineering Principle

One stage.

One objective.

One successful outcome.

---

# Final Statement

Stages transform engineering from a collection of tasks into a structured process.

They provide clarity, predictability, and continuous progress throughout the entire software development lifecycle.
