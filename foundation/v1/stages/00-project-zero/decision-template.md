# Decision Information

**Decision ID**

Unique identifier.

Example:

```
EDR-0001
```

---

**Project**

Project name.

---

**Stage**

Current engineering stage.

Example:

```
Stage 00 — Project Zero
```

---

**Decision Date**

YYYY-MM-DD

---

**Decision Owner**

Engineering Leader

---

# Decision Title

Provide a short and descriptive title.

Example:

```
Proceed with the Football Portal project.
```

---

# Context

Describe the situation that required a decision.

Include:

- Background
- Current understanding
- Relevant facts
- Existing constraints

The context should explain why this decision became necessary.

---

# Problem

Clearly define the problem being addressed.

Avoid describing solutions.

Focus on the engineering problem.

---

# Alternatives Considered

List every realistic alternative discussed.

For each alternative include:

- Description
- Advantages
- Disadvantages

Every important decision should compare options.

---

# Decision

Describe the selected alternative.

Explain what was decided.

---

# Rationale

Why was this decision chosen?

Support the decision with engineering reasoning.

Whenever possible, reference evidence instead of opinion.

---

# Expected Impact

Describe the expected consequences.

Consider:

- Technical impact
- Business impact
- Team impact
- User impact

---

# Risks

What risks remain after this decision?

Document known uncertainties.

---

# Follow-up Actions

List actions required after the decision.

Each action should have:

- Description
- Owner
- Expected completion stage

---

# Decision Status

Choose one:

- Proposed
- Approved
- Rejected
- Superseded

---

# Review Trigger

Under which conditions should this decision be reviewed?

Examples:

- New evidence becomes available.
- Requirements change.
- Technical constraints change.

Engineering decisions are not permanent.

They evolve with knowledge.

Engineering Leader

# Engineering Principle

Good engineering decisions are transparent, traceable, and revisitable.

The objective is not to avoid change.

The objective is to understand why change becomes necessary.
