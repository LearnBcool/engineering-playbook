# Project Zero — Meeting Agenda

> Foundation v1
> Stage 00

---

# Objective

Transform an initial idea into a clearly defined engineering problem.

The objective of this meeting is **not** to design solutions.

The objective is to create a shared understanding of the project.

---

# Meeting Rules

1. Understand before proposing solutions.
2. Challenge assumptions respectfully.
3. Document every important decision.
4. Avoid discussing technologies unless strictly necessary.
5. Every participant has the right to question unclear assumptions.

The Engineering Leader makes the final decision.

---

# Participants

Required

- Engineering Leader
- Problem Analyst
- Research Engineer
- Leadership Observer

Optional

- Domain Specialist
- Client Representative
- Product Owner

---

# Meeting Flow

## Step 1 — Project Presentation

The Engineering Leader presents the initial idea.

Questions:

- What is the idea?
- Why does it exist?
- Why now?

Deliverable:

Project Vision

---

## Step 2 — Problem Identification

The Problem Analyst leads the discussion.

Questions:

- What problem are we solving?
- Who experiences this problem?
- How is it solved today?
- Why is the current solution insufficient?

Deliverable:

Problem Statement

---

## Step 3 — Stakeholder Identification

Identify everyone affected by the project.

Questions:

- Who are the users?
- Who benefits?
- Who maintains the system?
- Who makes decisions?

Deliverable:

Stakeholder List

---

## Step 4 — Constraints

Identify known limitations.

Questions:

- Budget limitations?
- Time limitations?
- Technical limitations?
- Legal or regulatory constraints?
- Team limitations?

Deliverable:

Constraint Register

---

## Step 5 — Assumptions

List assumptions that still need validation.

Questions:

- What are we assuming?
- What evidence supports these assumptions?
- Which assumptions are risky?

Deliverable:

Assumption Register

---

## Step 6 — Risks

Identify the major project risks.

Questions:

- What could cause failure?
- What dependencies exist?
- What information is missing?

Deliverable:

Initial Risk Register

---

## Step 7 — Success Metrics

Define how success will be measured.

Questions:

- What does success look like?
- How will we measure it?
- When will we know the project is successful?

Deliverable:

Success Metrics

---

## Step 8 — Open Questions

List unanswered questions.

Every unanswered question becomes an action item.

Deliverable:

Open Questions Register

---

## Step 9 — Engineering Decision

The Engineering Leader summarizes the discussion.

Possible outcomes:

- GO
- NO-GO
- RESEARCH REQUIRED

GO

The project proceeds to Stage 01.

NO-GO

The project is rejected or postponed.

RESEARCH REQUIRED

Additional investigation is required before progressing.

---

# Meeting Deliverables

At the end of Project Zero the following artifacts must exist:

- Project Vision
- Problem Statement
- Stakeholder List
- Constraint Register
- Assumption Register
- Initial Risk Register
- Success Metrics
- Open Questions Register
- Engineering Decision

---

# Leadership Reflection

Before closing the meeting, the Leadership Observer answers:

- Was every participant heard?
- Were assumptions challenged?
- Were decisions supported by evidence?
- Did the Engineering Leader keep the discussion focused?
- Was consensus achieved when possible?
- Were disagreements documented?

Leadership is evaluated by the quality of the decision-making process.

---

# Engineering Principle

A successful Project Zero is measured by the quality of understanding, not by the number of decisions made.
