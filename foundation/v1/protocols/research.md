Stage 02 — Research

# Objective

Collect, evaluate, and synthesize existing knowledge relevant to the engineering problem.

The goal is to reduce uncertainty by learning from reliable evidence, prior work, industry practices, academic research, and real-world implementations.

Research informs decisions.

It does not make decisions.

---

# Trigger

Start automatically after:

- Discovery Report has been approved.
- Knowledge gaps have been identified.

---

# Participants

## Engineering Leader

Ensures research remains aligned with project objectives.

---

## Research Engineer

Leads all research activities.

Responsible for evidence quality and synthesis.

---

## Domain Specialist

Validates domain-specific findings.

---

## Problem Analyst

Verifies whether research addresses the identified problem.

---

## Leadership Observer

Evaluates collaboration, critical thinking, and decision discipline.

---

# Facilitator

Research Engineer

---

# Decision Authority

Engineering Leader

---

# Meeting Rules

- Evidence before opinion.
- Prefer primary sources when available.
- Distinguish facts from interpretations.
- Record uncertainty explicitly.
- Avoid selecting solutions prematurely.

---

# Discussion Flow

## Phase 1 — Research Objectives

Review the knowledge gaps identified during Discovery.

Expected Output:

Research Objectives

---

## Phase 2 — Source Identification

Identify reliable information sources.

Examples:

- Official documentation
- Scientific papers
- Technical books
- Standards
- Industry case studies
- Production systems
- Expert interviews

Expected Output:

Research Source List

---

## Phase 3 — Evidence Collection

Gather evidence related to each research objective.

Expected Output:

Evidence Register

---

## Phase 4 — Critical Evaluation

Evaluate each source.

Questions:

- Is the source trustworthy?
- Is it current?
- Is it applicable?
- What are its limitations?

Expected Output:

Evidence Assessment

---

## Phase 5 — Comparative Analysis

Compare different approaches.

Identify:

- Advantages
- Trade-offs
- Risks
- Costs
- Constraints

Expected Output:

Comparative Analysis Report

---

## Phase 6 — Knowledge Synthesis

Summarize the research.

Identify:

- Established knowledge
- Open questions
- Conflicting evidence
- Recommended areas for architecture exploration

Expected Output:

Research Report

---

## Phase 7 — Engineering Summary

Engineering Leader validates whether the research sufficiently reduces uncertainty.

Possible outcomes:

- READY FOR ARCHITECTURE
- ADDITIONAL RESEARCH REQUIRED

Expected Output:

Research Approval

---

# Conflict Resolution

If conflicting evidence exists:

1. Prefer higher-quality evidence.
2. Document competing viewpoints.
3. Record uncertainty.
4. Request additional investigation if necessary.

---

# Outputs

This protocol produces:

- Research Objectives
- Research Source List
- Evidence Register
- Evidence Assessment
- Comparative Analysis Report
- Research Report
- Research Approval

---

# Completion Criteria

The protocol is complete only when:

- Research objectives have been addressed.
- Evidence has been evaluated.
- Comparative analysis is documented.
- Remaining uncertainties are explicit.
- The Engineering Leader approves progression.

---

# Leadership Reflection

The Leadership Observer evaluates:

- Was evidence prioritized over opinion?
- Were sources critically evaluated?
- Did the team avoid confirmation bias?
- Were uncertainties documented?
- Did research remain aligned with the engineering problem?

---

# Engineering Principle

Research transforms information into engineering knowledge.

Engineering quality depends not on how much information is collected, but on how well evidence supports future decisions.
