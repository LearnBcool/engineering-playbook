> Discovery Protocol
> Foundation v1

# Protocol Information

**Protocol ID**

ECP-002

**Protocol Name**

Discovery

**Related Stage**

Stage 01 — Discovery

---

# Objective

Develop a deep understanding of the problem domain.

The purpose of Discovery is to understand the environment in which the problem exists, the people affected by it, and the factors that influence a successful solution.

Discovery expands understanding.

It does not search for solutions.

---

# Trigger

Start automatically after:

- Project Zero has been approved.
- The Engineering Decision Record (EDR) is marked as GO.

---

# Participants

## Engineering Leader

Facilitates the discussion and validates the outcomes.

---

## Problem Analyst

Leads the exploration of the problem domain.

---

## Domain Specialist

Provides domain-specific knowledge when required.

---

## Research Engineer

Identifies knowledge gaps that require future investigation.

---

## Leadership Observer

Evaluates collaboration, communication, and leadership during the meeting.

---

# Facilitator

Engineering Leader

---

# Decision Authority

Engineering Leader

---

# Meeting Rules

- Understand the domain before discussing solutions.
- Ask questions before making assumptions.
- Challenge ambiguity respectfully.
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Document everything that influences engineering decisions.

---

# Discussion Flow

## Phase 1 — Domain Exploration

Understand the domain where the project exists.

Questions:

- What industry or domain does the project belong to?
- How does this environment operate?
- What terminology is commonly used?

Expected Output:

Domain Overview

---

## Phase 2 — User Understanding

Identify the people affected.

Questions:

- Who are the primary users?
- What are their goals?
- What frustrations do they experience?
- What motivates them?

Expected Output:

User Profiles

---

## Phase 3 — Current Process

Understand how the problem is handled today.

Questions:

- What is the current workflow?
- What tools are used?
- Where are the bottlenecks?
- What causes inefficiency?

Expected Output:

Current Process Map

---

## Phase 4 — Pain Points

Identify opportunities for improvement.

Questions:

- What causes the greatest frustration?
- What wastes time or resources?
- What creates unnecessary complexity?

Expected Output:

Pain Point Register

---

## Phase 5 — Constraints

Identify domain-specific constraints.

Examples:

- Business
- Legal
- Operational
- Technical
- Financial

Expected Output:

Domain Constraints

---

## Phase 6 — Knowledge Gaps

Identify missing information.

Questions:

- What do we still not understand?
- What assumptions remain unverified?
- What requires research?

Expected Output:

Knowledge Gap Register

---

## Phase 7 — Discovery Summary

Engineering Leader summarizes the findings.

Expected Output:

Discovery Report

---

# Conflict Resolution

If disagreement occurs:

1. Clarify terminology.
2. Separate facts from assumptions.
3. Record competing interpretations.
4. Identify missing evidence.
5. Escalate unresolved issues to future research.

---

# Outputs

This protocol must produce:

- Domain Overview
- User Profiles
- Current Process Map
- Pain Point Register
- Domain Constraints
- Knowledge Gap Register
- Discovery Report

---

# Completion Criteria

The protocol is complete only when:

- The problem domain is understood.
- Users have been identified.
- Current processes are documented.
- Pain points are prioritized.
- Knowledge gaps are recorded.
- Discovery Report is approved.

---

# Leadership Reflection

The Leadership Observer evaluates:

- Did the team seek understanding before proposing solutions?
- Were assumptions challenged?
- Was every participant engaged?
- Was curiosity encouraged?
- Did the Engineering Leader maintain focus?

---

# Engineering Principle

Understanding the problem domain creates the foundation for meaningful engineering decisions.

Discovery reduces uncertainty about the environment.

Research reduces uncertainty about possible solutions.
