# Engineering Protocols

> Foundation v1

Engineering protocols define how engineering conversations happen.

A protocol standardizes communication between humans and AI agents, ensuring that discussions remain structured, objective, and focused on solving engineering problems.

Protocols do not define technical solutions.

Protocols define how engineering decisions are reached.

---

# Mission

Create repeatable, transparent, and high-quality engineering discussions.

A protocol ensures that every participant understands:

- Why the meeting exists.
- When to contribute.
- What decisions must be made.
- How disagreements are handled.
- When the discussion is complete.

---

# Core Principles

## Purpose Before Discussion

Every protocol begins with a clearly defined objective.

---

## Evidence Before Opinion

Engineering decisions should be supported by evidence whenever possible.

---

## Respectful Challenge

Participants are encouraged to question assumptions and proposals respectfully.

Disagreement is part of engineering.

---

## Explicit Decisions

Every important decision must be documented.

Nothing important should depend on memory.

---

## One Decision at a Time

A protocol should focus on a single engineering objective.

Avoid discussing unrelated topics.

---

# Protocol Structure

Every protocol follows the same structure.

- Objective
- Participants
- Roles
- Meeting Rules
- Discussion Flow
- Decision Process
- Outputs
- Completion Criteria

Consistency is essential.

---

# Human + AI Collaboration

Protocols are independent of implementation.

Whether participants are humans, AI agents, or hybrid teams, the protocol remains unchanged.

The protocol defines behavior.

The participants execute it.

---

# Engineering Principle

Great engineering begins with great conversations.

---

# Final Statement

Protocols transform engineering discussions into repeatable decision-making processes.

Every engineering conversation should follow a protocol.
